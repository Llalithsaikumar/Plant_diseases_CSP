Today, We visit a Cotton Farm to discuss common cotton plant diseases and potential solutions for prevention and treatment.

Diseases Discussed:

Ascochyta Blight: Fungal disease causes brown, sunken lesions on stems, leaves, and bolls, eventually leading to wilting and stunted growth.

Symptoms: During the visit, circular brown lesions with dark centers were observed on the lower leaves of some cotton plants.

Prevention: Crop rotation with non-susceptible crops like corn or alfalfa every 3-4 years was recommended as a preventative measure. Additionally, planting certified disease-resistant cotton varieties and maintaining proper field sanitation (removing plant debris after harvest) were discussed.

Treatment: As a preventative measure, applying a copper-based fungicide before the first visible symptoms appear was suggested. However, if symptoms are already present, treatment with a strobilurin fungicide might be necessary. The specific fungicide choice and application timing will depend on disease severity and local regulations.
